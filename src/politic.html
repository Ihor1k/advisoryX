1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how the Company collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data of individuals who visit our website, contact us, request information, or interact with our software and IT services.

The Company is committed to protecting your privacy and processing personal data in a transparent, lawful, and secure manner in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and applicable data protection legislation in Estonia.

The personal data we collect depends on how you interact with us. In most cases, we process only the information necessary to respond to inquiries, communicate with potential or existing clients, provide services, and maintain normal business operations.

2. What We Need

Our Personal Data Protection Policy governs the use and storage of personal data processed by the Company.

AdvisoryX OÜ acts as the controller of the personal data you provide to us through our website, by email, or through other direct communication channels. We collect only the personal data necessary for communication, service delivery, and business administration.

Personal data may include your name, company name, email address, telephone number, message content, and any other information you choose to provide when contacting us.

We may also process limited technical information related to your use of our website, such as IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, date and time of access, and similar usage data. This information is used to maintain website functionality, security, and performance.

Where our services involve software implementation, technical support, licensing, or custom development, we may process business contact details, project-related information, technical communication, access-related details, and other information required to provide the requested service.

3. Why We Need It

We process personal data to communicate with you, respond to inquiries, provide information about our services, and support potential or ongoing business relationships.

This may include using your contact details to answer questions, prepare service discussions, manage software-related requests, support licensing or implementation processes, provide technical assistance, and maintain records related to business communication.

The legal basis for processing your personal data may include your consent, the need to take steps before entering into a contract, the performance of a contract, compliance with legal obligations, and the legitimate interests of the Company.

Our legitimate interests may include maintaining communication with clients and partners, improving our services, protecting our website and systems, managing internal records, and supporting the normal operation of our business.

4. Who Receives Your Personal Data

Your personal data may be accessed by persons authorized by the Company, including employees, contractors, or service providers who need such access to perform their duties.

Where necessary, we may share personal data with third parties that help us operate our website, provide IT infrastructure, deliver software-related services, support communication, manage accounting, or comply with legal and administrative obligations.

Such third parties may include hosting providers, IT service providers, software vendors, licensing partners, professional advisers, accounting service providers, or public authorities where disclosure is required by law.

We do not sell your personal data. Any party that processes personal data on our behalf is expected to protect it and process it only in accordance with applicable data protection requirements.

5. Transfer of Personal Data to Third Countries

In certain cases, personal data may be transferred to or accessed from countries outside the European Economic Area.

Such transfers may occur when we use service providers, software vendors, cloud platforms, or technical tools that are located outside the European Economic Area or store data on international infrastructure.

Where such transfers take place, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are applied in accordance with applicable data protection legislation. These safeguards may include adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, contractual obligations, or other lawful transfer mechanisms under the GDPR.

6. How Long We Keep Your Personal Information

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected and processed.

Contact and inquiry data may be retained for as long as needed to respond to your request and manage related communication. Business and service-related data may be retained for the duration of the business relationship and for a reasonable period afterwards where necessary for contractual, accounting, legal, or legitimate business purposes.

In certain cases, personal data may be kept for a longer period if required by law, for the establishment or defense of legal claims, or for internal record-keeping connected to services provided by the Company.

When personal data is no longer needed, we delete it or anonymize it where appropriate.

7. Your Data Protection Rights

You have the right to request access to your personal data and to receive information about how it is processed.

You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. You may also request deletion of your personal data where there is no valid legal reason for us to continue processing it. You may have the right to request restriction of processing, object to processing, and request data portability where applicable. Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.

Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.
